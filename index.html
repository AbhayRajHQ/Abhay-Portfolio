The Story

I grew up in a village where technology felt distant — something that existed elsewhere, for other people. But curiosity doesn't wait for access. Even before I had the tools, I was fascinated by how things worked.

When I moved to the city for education, I saw the gap clearly — the competition, the exposure, the pace. It could have been intimidating. Instead, it became fuel. That contrast taught me more about drive than any classroom could.

I'm not the fastest. I'm not the loudest. But I show up, consistently. I build, I break things, I learn why, and I rebuild better. That's the loop I live in — and it's the only one I trust.
